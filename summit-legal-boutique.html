Our Story

Founded on the belief that less can be more.

Summit Legal Boutique was founded in 2003 by James Ashworth and Rachel Ngata, two former partners at one of New Zealand's largest commercial law firms. They shared a frustration: that in large firms, the partners who win the work aren't always the ones doing it.

They left to build something deliberately small and deliberately excellent. A firm where every client relationship is held by the people with the deepest expertise. Where no matter is too small for a partner's attention, and no transaction too complex for our focused team to handle.

Two decades later, Summit Legal has advised on over $2.4 billion in transactions, guided dozens of New Zealand companies into international markets, and built a reputation as the firm that Wellington's sharpest business minds turn to when the stakes are highest.

We remain deliberately boutique — not because we can't grow, but because staying small is how we stay exceptional.

Mergers & Acquisitions

Transactions

Our M&A practice is the cornerstone of the firm. We advise buyers, sellers, and boards on transactions ranging from founder-led trade sales to complex multi-jurisdictional acquisitions. Our team has closed over 120 transactions in the past decade alone.

What sets us apart is continuity. The partner who scopes your deal is the same one reviewing the SPA at 2am. There's no handoff to a junior team at the eleventh hour. You get consistent strategic thinking from term sheet to completion.

Due Diligence Deal Structuring Sale & Purchase Agreements Post-Completion Cross-Border M&A
02

Corporate Governance

Advisory

Good governance isn't bureaucracy — it's the framework that lets ambitious companies move fast without breaking things. We help boards establish structures that protect their interests while enabling decisive action.

From constitution drafting to boardroom disputes, our governance team brings a practical sensibility. We've advised boards through IPO preparation, investor activism, and complex multi-stakeholder situations where the right governance framework made the difference.

Board Advisory Shareholder Agreements Director Duties Constitutions Disputes & Remedies
03

Commercial Contracts

Transactions

Every significant business relationship is built on a contract. We draft, negotiate, and advise on the agreements that underpin our clients' most important commercial arrangements — from long-term supply agreements to complex technology licensing deals.

Our approach is pragmatic. We understand that a contract that sits unsigned because it's "legally perfect" serves no one. We find the balance between robust protection and commercial reality, and we do it efficiently.

Supply Agreements Technology & IP Licensing Distribution Franchise Joint Ventures
04

Capital Raising & Private Equity

Growth

Whether you're a startup closing a seed round or an established company seeking institutional capital, the legal structure of your raise matters enormously. We've advised on over 60 capital raises, from $500K angel rounds to $150M private equity transactions.

We act for both companies raising capital and investors deploying it, which gives us a rare dual perspective. We know what experienced investors look for, and we structure raises that give both sides confidence.

Seed & Series Funding Private Equity Convertible Notes SAFE Agreements Investor Terms
05

International Trade & Expansion

Cross-Border

New Zealand companies increasingly look beyond our borders for growth. We help them navigate the legal complexity of international expansion — from establishing overseas entities to navigating foreign regulatory regimes and structuring cross-border transactions.

Through our network of specialist firms across Australia, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific, we coordinate multi-jurisdictional matters without the overhead of a global firm. Our clients get local expertise in each market, coordinated by a team that already knows their business.

Overseas Structuring Trade Compliance Regulatory Navigation Cross-Border JVs Treaty Obligations
06

Regulatory & Compliance

Risk

New Zealand's regulatory environment is evolving rapidly. From new privacy legislation to strengthened anti-money laundering requirements, businesses face an increasingly complex compliance landscape. We help our clients stay ahead of these changes rather than reacting to them.

Our regulatory team includes a former Commerce Commission advisor and a specialist in financial services regulation, giving us practical insight into how regulators think and act.

Privacy & Data AML/CFT Financial Regulation Commerce Act Licensing
